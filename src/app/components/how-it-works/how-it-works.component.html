How It Works

The difficulty in creating a collaborative text editting software system is caused by the latency between clients. Because of the time it takes for a user's change to the document model to propogate to other clients, conflicts can arise and the clients will fall out of sync. This issue is best illustrated with a simple example:

In this example we have two clients: Amy and Bob. They are editting a document that currently as "abc" as the document model. Amy enters the character '@' at the beginning of the document. On her screen, she will see the document model update to "@abc". Her change to the document will then be sent to Bob's client, however it will take awhile for the message to travel to him. At near the same time, Bob inserts character '%' at the beginning of the document. Bob's document model will now read as "%abc". Bob's change will then be sent to Amy's document.

When Amy's change finally arrives to Bob, it sees that Bob's document model is "%abc". Remember that Amy's change was insert '@' at the beginning of the document, or index 0. When Amy's change is applied to Bob's document, Bob's final document model is "@%abc". However, when Bob's initial change of insert '%' to the beginning of the document arrives and is applied to Amy's document, Amy ends up with a document state of "%@abc".

Amy and Bob's documents are now different. This is considered a confliction in the document state and is why managing real-time document editting systems is difficult.
